# Skill: Trust Architecture for Fintech About Pages

## Skill Type
Brand Strategy / Conversion Architecture / Trust Design

---

## Purpose

Design an About page that increases:
- Perceived legitimacy
- Perceived competence
- Perceived safety
- Perceived stability

In fintech, the About page is a **trust engine**, not a biography.

---

## Core Principle

Users are subconsciously asking:

1. Is this company real?
2. Do they know what they’re doing?
3. Is my money safe?

Every section must answer one of these.

---

## The Trust Layering Framework

### 1. Clarity Layer
- State clearly what you do.
- Define who you serve.
- Define the financial problem you solve.
- Avoid abstraction and buzzwords.

Rule: If a 15-year-old cannot understand it, rewrite it.

---

### 2. Credibility Layer
- Show relevant founder or leadership experience.
- Highlight financial or technical expertise.
- Use specific facts, not claims.
- Include real photos.

Rule: Specificity increases believability.

---

### 3. Safety Layer (Non-Negotiable in Fintech)
- State regulatory status clearly.
- Mention licensing and jurisdiction.
- Explain how funds are protected.
- Clarify data security standards.
- Be transparent about risk.

Rule: Surface safety early, not in the footer.

---

### 4. Proof Layer
- Show metrics (users, volume, growth, AUM).
- Include partnerships or investors.
- Add credible press mentions.
- Quantify impact wherever possible.

Rule: Numbers > adjectives.

---

### 5. Stability Layer
- Communicate long-term vision.
- Show roadmap direction.
- Signal institutional seriousness.

Rule: Money does not trust experiments.

---

## Structural Rules

- Start with what you do, not your origin story.
- Keep paragraphs short and scannable.
- Avoid overhyped or crypto-style language.
- Use restrained, precise tone.
- Remove anything that does not increase trust.

---

## Validation Test

For each section, ask:

> Does this increase perceived legitimacy, competence, or safety?

If not, remove or rewrite it.

---

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Founder diary storytelling
- Abstract mission statements
- Hiding compliance information
- No real team presence
- Vague claims like “redefining finance”
- Long unstructured text blocks

---

## Mental Model

About Page =  
Trust Architecture × Risk Reduction × Authority Signaling
